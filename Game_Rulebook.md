# Borderland Arena - Official Game Documentation & Rulebook

## 1. Overview & Team Format
Welcome to **Borderland Arena**, an intense, multi-stage coding and puzzle-solving competition under high-pressure conditions.
- **Team Size:** Players participate in teams (size based on event rules, typically 2-3 members).
- **Collaboration:** Teammates must collaborate, delegate tasks, and strategize within the time limits of each round.
- **Account:** All members of a team will play under a single registered team account 

## 2. General Rules & Conduct
- **Fair Play:** Teams are expected to maintain integrity throughout the event. Any use of unfair means, plagiarism, unauthorized internet searches (unless specified), or tampering with the platform will not be tolerated.
- **Warning System:** If a team receives **2 warnings**, they will be **immediately disqualified** from the event. Warnings can be issued by administrators or triggered automatically by the anti-cheat system.
- **Anti-Cheat Environment (Atmospheric Breach):** Players must remain on the active game tab during the rounds. The platform's anti-cheat mechanisms monitor for window focus loss, unauthorized tab switching, or external assistance. Triggering this system will log a breach, notify the admins, and may result in a warning.

## 3. Leaderboard & Scoring System
- **Real-time Leaderboard:** The leaderboard updates dynamically throughout the event as teams submit answers and complete rounds. 
- **Ranking Criteria:** Teams are ranked primarily by their **Total Score**.
- **Tie-breakers:** In the event of a tie in points, the team that completed the questions/rounds in the shortest amount of accumulated time will be ranked higher. (Speed and efficiency matter).
- **Admin Review:** Administrators monitor the leaderboard and have the right to adjust scores or apply penalties manually based on rule violations or disputes.

---

## 4. Game Structure & Rounds

The arena consists of four distinct rounds, scaling in difficulty and format.

### Round 1: Entry Game (MCQ)
- **Duration:** 10 minutes
- **Questions:** 50
- **Points per Question:** 10 points
- **Total Possible Points:** 500 points
- **Description:** A fast-paced multiple-choice (MCQ) and short-input format testing foundational knowledge, standard trivia, or basic coding concepts. Speed, quick thinking, and accuracy are critical.

### Round 2: Execution Trace
- **Duration:** 20 minutes
- **Questions:** 25
- **Points per Question:** 20 points
- **Total Possible Points:** 500 points
- **Description:** Teams are presented with complex code snippets and must mentally trace the code's execution. Players must determine and input the exact "decrypted output" the code would produce. This tests deeper analytical and algorithmic tracing skills without writing code.

### Round 3: Reverse Compiler
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Questions:** 5
- **Points per Question:** 100 points
- **Total Possible Points:** 500 points
- **Score Evaluation:**
  - Submissions involve writing functional code to solve algorithmic problems from scratch.
  - **The Twist:** The code editor mechanically types in reverse, placing extreme cognitive load on the operatives as they build their logic.
  - Answers are compiled and evaluated against multiple hidden test cases in real-time. Full or partial points are awarded based on the proportion of test cases passed.

### Round 4: Code Autopsy
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Questions:** 5
- **Points per Question:** 100 points
- **Total Possible Points:** 500 points
- **Score Evaluation:**
  - This final round features complex debugging and optimization problems. Teams are provided with broken, inefficient, or incomplete code and must patch it correctly.
  - **Dynamic Code Evaluation:** The platform executes your modifications remotely against invisible test cases.
  - **Efficiency Penalty:** A strict penalty is applied based on the number of lines changed. To get maximum points, teams must deploy optimal, surgical patches rather than brute-force rewrites.
